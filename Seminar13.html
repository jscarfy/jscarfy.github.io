Seminar Goal: We will aim to cover as much as possible about the basic theory of elliptic curves, the first goal is to understand the group structure on the rational points of an elliptic curve and Mordell-Weil theorem. Simultaneously, we shall also study the algebraic geometric aspects as well as some arithmetic aspects of the theory of elliptic curves.

Prerequisites: Basic knowledge of Algebra (e.g. Math 322 at UBC) and Arithmetic (e.g. Math 312 at UBC). Some knowledge of commutative algebra and slightly more advanced topics in number theory will be useful but not essential.

Evaluation: Each registered student is expected to give a few lectures on an assigned topic. Grade will be based on the quality of lectures and regular attendance.

References for these topics:

  • J. H. Sliverman and J. Tate, Rational Points on Elliptic Curves
  • J. W. Cassels, Lectures on Elliptic Curves
